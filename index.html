About Us

Mr. David A. Stoner

David A. Stoner has a long history in the renewables and independent power industry. Dave has extensive experience in developing independent power projects, having managed the successful development to completion of over 3000 MW of independent power projects fueled by solar, wind, natural gas and coal since the early 1990’s.

Dave currently manages and is principal of his own consulting business to the energy industry, Stoner Power Consulting, LLC. In this capacity he provides strategic, project development, and M&A services to various clients in the independent power industry primarily related to solar, wind, and energy storage projects. Dave’s expertise includes development of market strategies, project siting, wind and solar resources assessment, licensing, power purchase agreement negations, interconnections studies, equipment procurement and EPC contractor selection, partner and M&A negotiations, and project financing. Prior to forming his own consulting firm, he lead development teams and directed development of numerous solar, wind, gas-fired, coal-fired, biomass, and IGCC generation projects in the following roles:

  • Senior Vice President-Development with Element Power
  • Senior Vice President-Development with BP Alternative Energy
  • Director Development with Greenlight Energy
  • Vice President-Development with Newport Generation Ventures, Inc.
  • Vice President and Managing Director for Entergy in Europe
  • Vice President-Development of Westmoreland Energy, Inc.

David started his career in the energy business with Pennsylvania Power and Light Company in the early 1980’s. He received a B.S. degree in environmental management from the Pennsylvania State University in 1983. Dave also earned a M.S. degree in civil engineering from Lehigh University in 1989.

Becca Stoner

Becca Stoner currently serves as a project developer for Stoner Power Consulting, LLC. Becca provides a variety of services including project siting and constraints analyses, conceptual design, permitting, mapping, landowner outreach, and real estate research. She has extensive experience using GIS to aid in project development.

Becca attended the University of Virginia and received a B.S. degree in civil engineering in 2015. She worked as a water resources engineer for Arcadis for three years where she became experienced in local and state permit requirements for development, specifically of stormwater and water treatment projects.
